# How to Split or Merge PDF Pages — Complete Guide

You have a 50-page PDF. You only need pages 3 through 7.

Or you have five separate PDFs — invoices, reports, and appendices — and you need them as one clean document.

These aren't edge cases. They're daily workflows for millions of people. And you don't need Adobe Acrobat to do them.

Here's how to split and merge PDFs for free, right in your browser — no uploads, no sign-up, no software install.

## Split PDF: Extract What You Need

### When Would You Split a PDF?

- **Send one chapter** — extract pages 12-24 from a 200-page report
- **Separate invoices** — one combined PDF of 30 invoices → 30 individual files
- **Remove blank pages** — split, delete blanks, merge the rest
- **Share only relevant pages** — don't send confidential pages by accident
- **Reduce file size** — split a large PDF into manageable chunks for email

### Method 1: Split All Pages

This gives you every page as a separate PDF file:

1. Go to the **[Split PDF tool](/tools/split-pdf)**
2. Upload your PDF (drag & drop or click to browse)
3. Click **Split All Pages**
4. Download individual pages — each named with its original page number

This is perfect when you need to reorganize a document or extract specific pages to use elsewhere.

### Method 2: Extract a Page Range

Need pages 5-10 from a larger document?

1. Go to the **[Split PDF tool](/tools/split-pdf)**
2. Upload your PDF
3. Enter the page range (e.g., `5-10`) in the range input
4. Click to extract — you get one PDF containing only those pages

**Examples of page ranges:**

| You want | You enter |
|----------|-----------|
| Pages 3 through 7 | `3-7` |
| Just page 1 | `1-1` |
| Pages 10 to the end | `10-` |
| First 5 pages | `-5` |

### Method 3: Split by Size

Need to split a large PDF into smaller chunks for email? Split all pages first, then use the **[Merge PDF tool](/tools/merge-pdf)** to combine pages into groups of 5 or 10. More on this below.

## Merge PDF: Combine Everything into One

### When Would You Merge PDFs?

- **Monthly reports** — combine weekly reports into one monthly document
- **Job applications** — merge resume + cover letter + portfolio into one PDF
- **Invoice batch** — send all invoices as one file to accounting
- **Meeting prep** — combine agenda + reports + slides into one briefing packet
- **Study materials** — merge lecture notes, slides, and readings by topic

### How to Merge PDFs (30 Seconds)

1. Go to the **[Merge PDF tool](/tools/merge-pdf)**
2. Upload all the PDFs you want to combine
3. **Drag to arrange them in order** — the first file becomes page 1
4. Click **Merge PDFs**
5. Download your combined PDF

The tool preserves everything from your original files — fonts, images, formatting, links, and page sizes. Even if your PDFs have different page dimensions (A4 mixed with Letter), each page keeps its original size.

### Merge Tips

- **Naming matters** — rename your files before uploading (e.g., `01-Cover.pdf`, `02-Report.pdf`, `03-Appendix.pdf`) so they upload in the right order
- **Preview before merging** — check that you have all files and they're in the right order
- **Merge in batches** — combining 50+ PDFs? Merge in groups of 10-15, then merge the groups. Easier to catch ordering mistakes.

## Common Questions

**Q: Is there a file size limit?**
A: No hard limit — processing is done in your browser. Very large PDFs (200MB+) may be slow depending on your device. For best results, keep files under 100MB.

**Q: Can I split a password-protected PDF?**
A: The split tool works with unprotected PDFs. Remove the password first, then split.

**Q: Will merging change my formatting?**
A: No. The merge tool uses PDF-lib which preserves fonts, images, layout, and links from your original PDFs exactly as they are.

**Q: Can I merge PDFs with different page sizes (A4 + Letter)?**
A: Yes. Each page keeps its original dimensions in the merged output. There's no forced resizing.

**Q: How do I remove specific pages from a PDF?**
A: Split all pages → download only the pages you want to keep → optionally merge them back into one PDF.

**Q: Can I merge 100 PDFs at once?**
A: Technically yes, but for large merges, do it in batches of 10-15 files. This makes it easier to verify the order is correct before the final merge.
