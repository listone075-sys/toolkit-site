# How to Create QR Codes for Free (URL, WiFi, Text, vCard) — No Sign-Up

QR codes are everywhere — restaurant menus, WiFi logins, event tickets, payment links. But creating one shouldn't require downloading software or signing up for yet another service. Here's how to generate QR codes for free, in seconds, with nothing leaving your browser.

## What Can You Put in a QR Code?

QR codes can encode many types of data, not just URLs:

| Content Type | Example | Use Case |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **URL** | `https://yourwebsite.com` | Share a link, portfolio, product page |
| **WiFi** | SSID + password | Let guests connect without typing |
| **Text** | Any plain text message | Instructions, notes, scavenger hunt clues |
| **Email** | `mailto:hello@example.com` | Pre-fill an email address for users |
| **vCard** | Contact card data | Share your business card digitally |
| **Phone** | `tel:+1234567890` | Tap to call a number |

## Pro Tips for Better QR Codes

### 1. Size Matters
- **Print**: At least 300×300 pixels (1 inch × 1 inch at 300 DPI)
- **Screen**: At least 200×200 pixels
- **Billboards**: Vector format if possible, or 1000+ pixels

### 2. Choose the Right Colors
- High contrast is essential — dark foreground on light background
- Test print before mass production — some color combinations scan poorly
- Avoid inverted colors (white QR on dark background) unless testing confirms it works

### 3. Leave a Quiet Zone
- Keep at least 4 modules of blank space around the QR code
- Don't place text or logos too close to the edges

### 4. Test Before Printing
- Test with at least 3 different phone models (iPhone, Android, older devices)
- Test from different distances (close-up and arm's reach)
- Test under the actual lighting conditions where the QR code will be used

### 5. Add a Logo (Carefully)
- A small center logo can increase brand recognition
- Keep it under 15% of the total QR code area
- The QR code has built-in error correction — small obstructions are OK

## Common QR Code Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Too small | Phone camera can't focus | Minimum 200×200px for screen, 300×300px for print |
| Low contrast | Scanner can't distinguish modules | Dark foreground + light background |
| Broken link | URL changed or expired | Test before printing; use redirectable shortlinks |
| No WiFi, only SSID | Users still need to type password | Always include WiFi password in the QR data |
| Printed on curved surface | Distortion prevents scanning | Print larger and test on the actual surface |

## When to Use QR Codes vs Other Options

| Scenario | Best Option | Why |
|----------|------------|-----|
| Restaurant menu | QR code → URL | Replaces physical menus, easy to update |
| WiFi sharing at home | QR code → WiFi | Guests scan and connect instantly |
| Business card | QR code → vCard | No typing contact details manually |
| Event ticket | QR code → unique URL | Can be validated at entry |
| Product packaging | QR code → landing page | Trackable, updatable after printing |
| Long-term signage | Short URL + QR | URL may change; redirect the shortlink |
