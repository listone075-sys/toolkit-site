# Best Image Format for Social Media 2026

Posting the wrong image format on social media means blurry photos, slow loading, or broken previews in a pinned tweet. Each platform has quirks — here's exactly what works, and the tools to get it right.

## Quick Reference — Format by Platform

| Platform | Best Format | Max Size | Aspect Ratio |
|----------|:-----------:|:--------:|:------------:|
| Instagram Feed | **JPEG** | 10 MB | 1:1, 4:5, 1.91:1 |
| Instagram Stories | **JPEG** | 4 MB | 9:16 |
| Twitter/X Feed | **PNG** (text) / **JPEG** (photos) | 5 MB | 16:9, 1:1 |
| Pinterest Pins | **PNG** | 20 MB | **2:3 (vertical)** |
| LinkedIn Feed | **JPEG** | 20 MB | 1.91:1, 1:1 |
| Facebook Feed | **JPEG** | 30 MB | 1.91:1, 4:5, 1:1 |

> 💡 **Pro tip:** PNG is better when your image has text, logos, or screenshots. JPEG is better for photos. The file size limits above are per-platform — compress before uploading to avoid quality loss from auto-compression.

## Instagram

Instagram re-compresses everything you upload, so start with a high-quality source.

- **Feed posts:** JPEG, quality 90%, sRGB color profile
- **Stories:** JPEG, quality 85%, 1080×1920 px
- **Carousels:** All images should be the same aspect ratio (Instagram will crop otherwise)
- **Avoid PNG for photos:** Instagram's JPEG compression is aggressive — PNG won't look better and takes longer to upload

**Recommended tool:** Use the [Image Compressor](https://toolcraftbox.com/tools/image-compressor) to balance quality and file size, then the [Image Resizer](https://toolcraftbox.com/tools/image-resizer) for exact dimensions.

## Twitter / X

Twitter is more forgiving with formats, but file size matters — large images get auto-compressed to look terrible.

- **Photos in tweets:** JPEG at 80-85% quality — keeps it under 5 MB automatically
- **Screenshots with text:** **PNG** is non-negotiable. JPEG compression destroys text readability
- **Profile / cover images:** JPEG, 400×400 for avatar, 1500×500 for header
- **Card previews for link sharing:** Twitter prefers **JPEG** for link previews. Use the [Image Cropper](https://toolcraftbox.com/tools/image-cropper) to get the right 2:1 ratio for link cards

> 🔍 Twitter displays images at up to 1600×1900 px in the timeline. Larger files get downsized, not cropped — but they also take longer to load. 1200×675 px (16:9) is the sweet spot.

## Pinterest

Pinterest is unique — it's a visual search engine, not just social media. Image quality directly affects your reach.

- **Format:** **Always PNG** — Pinterest serves high-resolution images in search results and cropped previews
- **Aspect ratio:** 2:3 (1000×1500 px) is the gold standard. Taller pins perform better
- **File size:** Under 20 MB (PNG at 1000×1500 px at 72 DPI is usually ~2-5 MB)
- **Text overlay:** Add text on the image itself. PNG keeps text sharp
- **Don't upload JPEG for Pins:** Pinterest re-compresses JPEGs more aggressively than PNGs

**Pro tip:** If your source image is a different format, convert it first. Use [WebP to JPG](https://toolcraftbox.com/tools/webp-to-jpg) or [PNG to JPG](https://toolcraftbox.com/tools/png-to-jpg) (or vice versa) to get the right starting format before resizing.

## LinkedIn

LinkedIn handles images better than most platforms, but has strict size limits.

- **Feed images:** JPEG, 1200×627 px (1.91:1) for shared link previews
- **Company page covers:** JPEG, 1128×191 px — surprisingly small
- **Documents / PDFs:** LinkedIn supports document uploads, but images in the feed get 2× more engagement
- **Keep it under 5 MB:** LinkedIn's upload is slow above this

## Facebook

- **Feed:** JPEG, up to 2048 px wide. Facebook compresses everything below 85% quality anyway
- **Event covers:** JPEG, 1920×1080 px
- **Profile covers:** JPEG, 820×462 px (desktop) / 640×360 px (mobile)
- **360° photos:** JPEG only (Facebook expects equirectangular projection)

## FAQ

### Does WebP work on social media?

Most platforms **accept** WebP uploads, but they often convert it to JPEG internally anyway. Stick to JPEG or PNG for best quality control.

### What about AVIF?

AVIF is supported by some platforms (Chrome-based browsers) but not all. Twitter and Instagram convert AVIF to JPEG on upload. Stick to JPEG for now — AVIF adoption will grow in 2027+.

### Should I use sRGB or Adobe RGB?

**Always sRGB** for social media. Adobe RGB looks great in Photoshop but gets washed out everywhere else. Most phones and all social platforms expect sRGB.

### Why are my images blurry after uploading?

Three most common causes:
1. **Too large** — the platform downsized it aggressively (compress to 80-90% quality first)
2. **Wrong aspect ratio** — the platform cropped it automatically (crop to the platform's preferred ratio)
3. **Interlaced/progressive JPEG** — some platforms handle these poorly (save as baseline JPEG)

### Does image format affect engagement?

Yes. Pinterest explicitly favors high-quality PNGs in its algorithm. Twitter images with text (which need PNG for readability) get 2-3× more clicks. Instagram treats all formats equally but loads JPEG faster — and faster loading = higher likelihood of people stopping to view.
