# How to Convert PDF to JPG or PNG

You have a PDF and you need an image. Maybe you want to share just one page on social media, embed a document preview on your website, or pull a chart into a presentation. Whatever the reason — here's how to convert PDF pages to JPG or PNG for free.

## Why Convert PDF to Image?

- **Share a single page** — instead of sending a 20-page PDF, send one image
- **Embed in websites** — browsers display images natively, PDFs need a viewer
- **Use in presentations** — PowerPoint and Google Slides handle images better than PDFs
- **Post on social media** — Instagram, Twitter, and Pinterest don't accept PDF uploads
- **Extract graphics** — pull charts, diagrams, and illustrations out of PDFs

## JPG vs PNG — Which Format to Choose

| Scenario | Best Format | Why |
|----------|:-----------:|-----|
| Photographs and images | **JPG** | Smaller file size, designed for photos |
| Text-heavy pages | **PNG** | Crisp text, no compression artifacts |
| Screenshots | **PNG** | Sharp lines, exact reproduction |
| Sharing via email/chat | **JPG** | Smaller = faster to send |
| Archiving documents | **PNG** | Lossless quality, future-proof |
| Social media posts | **JPG** | Platforms expect JPG, smaller files |
| Printing | **PNG** | Higher quality output |

> 💡 **Pro tip:** Not sure which to use? Start with PNG for the best quality. If the file size is too large, switch to JPG at 90% quality — you'll barely notice the difference.
